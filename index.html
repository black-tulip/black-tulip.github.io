Dividing Water: Protests, Riots, and Water Conflict in India

Two weak monsoons and the ever increasing demands of a vibrant developing economy have left India facing its most severe water crisis in recent memory. According the The Economist 330 million people are facing acute water shortages. The fallout ranges from local governments unable to provide potable water to power plants forced to shut down because the rivers that provide essential cooling are no longer flowing. As this critical resource dries up, increased competition breeds conflict between residents, farmers, industry, local governments, and other communities. Instances of water related conflict are increasing.

This map was created with leaflet with data hosted in CartoDB.

  • See the map: Water Conflict in India
  • Data Source: Armed Conflict Location and Event Dataset (Asia)
  • Data Source: Aqueduct Water Risk Atlas
